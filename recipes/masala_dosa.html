Crispy Masala Dosa

Description

Masala Dosa- Masala Dosa, a South Indian staple, features crispy fermented crepes filled with a flavorful potato masala.

This recipe guides you through making delicious homemade dosa from scratch.

Ingredients:

For Dosa Batter

  • 2 cups long-grain rice (like basmati)
  • ½ cup urad dal (black gram lentils)
  • 1 teaspoon fenugreek seeds
  • ¾ teaspoon salt (or to taste)
  • Water

For the Potato Masala:

  • 2 medium potatoes, boiled and mashed
  • 1 medium onion, finely chopped
  • 1 green chili pepper, finely chopped (optional)
  • 1 inch ginger, grated
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
  • ½ teaspoon mustard seeds
  • ½ teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1 sprig curry leaves
  • ½ teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  • ¼ teaspoon red chili powder (adjust to your spice preference)
  • Salt to taste
  • Cilantro leaves, chopped (for garnish)

Instructions:

  1. Soak the Rice and Dal:

    In a large bowl, combine the rinsed rice, urad dal, and fenugreek seeds.

    Wash them together thoroughly. Add enough water to cover them by about 2 inches. Soak for at least 6-8 hours, or preferably overnight.

  2. Grind the Batter:

    After soaking, drain the water from the rice and dal mixture. Grind it into a smooth batter using a blender or grinder.

    Add water gradually while grinding to achieve a slightly thick, pourable consistency (like thin pancake batter).

    Add the salt and mix well. Cover the batter and let it ferment in a warm place for 8-10 hours, or until it doubles in volume and becomes slightly bubbly.

  3. Prepare the Masala:

    In a pan, heat oil over medium heat. Add the mustard seeds and cumin seeds.

    When they begin to splutter, add the curry leaves. Let them crackle for a few seconds.

  4. Sauté the Aromatics:

    Add the chopped onion, green chili pepper (if using), ginger, and garlic to the pan.

    Sauté until the onions become translucent and softened, about 5 minutes.

  5. Spice it Up:

    Add the turmeric powder, coriander powder, and red chili powder to the pan.

    Stir and cook for a minute, allowing the spices to release their aroma.

  6. Incorporate the Potatoes:

    Add the mashed potatoes and salt to the pan. Mix well and cook for 5-7 minutes,

    until the filling is heated through and flavors are combined. Mash any large potato pieces for a smoother texture.

  7. Cook the Dosa:

    Heat a flat griddle or non-stick pan over medium heat. Once hot,

    dip a ladle or measuring cup into the fermented batter and swirl it to coat the bottom of the pan thinly and evenly.

    Drizzle a few drops of oil around the edges of the dosa.

  8. Crisp it Up:

    Let the dosa cook for a minute or two, until the bottom becomes slightly golden brown and the edges start to loosen.

  9. Fill and Fold:

    Spread a spoonful of the prepared potato masala in the center of the cooked dosa.

    Use a spatula to gently fold the dosa in half, like a crescent moon. Press down slightly to secure the filling.

  10. Serve Hot:

    Cook for another minute or two on both sides, until the dosa is crispy and golden brown.

    Slide the masala dosa onto a plate and serve hot with coconut chutney or sambar (a lentil and vegetable stew), and garnish with chopped cilantro leaves.

Tips:

  • To ensure crispy dosas, make sure your griddle or pan is hot enough before cooking.

  • If the batter seems too thick, add a little water to thin it out. Conversely, if it's too thin, let it ferment for a longer time.

  • You can adjust the amount of chili powder depending on your desired level of spice.

  • Leftover dosa batter can be stored in the refrigerator for up to 2-3 days.
