Crispy Samosa

Description

Samosa- Samosa are a beloved Indian snack or appetizer, featuring crispy fried pastry filled with a savory potato and pea mixture.

This recipe creates delicious homemade samosa that are sure to impress.

Ingredients:

For the Dough:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 cup vegetable oil, plus extra for frying
  • 1/2 cup water (approximately)

For the Filling:

  • 2 medium potatoes, boiled and mashed
  • 1/2 cup frozen peas, thawed
  • 1 medium onion, finely chopped
  • 1 green chili pepper, finely chopped (optional)
  • 1 inch ginger, grated
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon cumin powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon red chili powder (adjust to your spice preference)
  • Salt to taste
  • Cilantro leaves, chopped (for garnish)

Instructions:

  1. Make the Dough:

    In a large bowl, combine the flour and salt. Add the vegetable oil and mix it in using your fingertips until it resembles coarse crumbs.

    Gradually add water, a tablespoon at a time, kneading until a soft and pliable dough forms. You might not need all the water.

  2. Knead and Rest:

    Knead the dough for 5-7 minutes on a lightly floured surface until smooth and elastic.

    Cover the dough with a damp cloth and let it rest for at least 30 minutes.

  3. Prepare the Filling:

    In a large pan, heat a tablespoon of vegetable oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and green chili pepper (if using) and saute until softened and translucent.

    Add the ginger and garlic and cook for another minute, until fragrant.

  4. Spice it Up:

    Add the garam masala powder, cumin powder, turmeric powder, and red chili powder to the pan.

    Stir and cook for a minute, allowing the spices to release their aroma.

  5. Incorporate the Vegetables:

    Add the mashed potatoes, peas, and salt to the pan. Mix well and cook for 5-7 minutes, until the filling is heated through and flavors are combined.

    Remove the pan from the heat and let the filling cool slightly.

  6. Assemble the Samosas:

    Divide the dough into equal portions and roll each portion into a thin circle on a lightly floured surface.

    Use a saucer or cookie cutter to cut out circles from the dough.

  7. Fill and Fold:

    Place a spoonful of the cooled filling in the center of each dough circle. Moisten the edges with a little water to seal.

    Fold the circle into a half-moon shape, pressing the edges firmly to seal. You can also fold them into triangle shapes.

  8. Heat the Oil:

    Heat enough oil in a deep pan or fryer for frying. The ideal temperature is around 180°C (350°F).

  9. Fry the Samosas:

    Gently slide the samosas into the hot oil and fry in batches until golden brown and crispy on all sides, about 3-4 minutes per batch.

  10. Drain and Serve:

    Remove the fried samosas from the oil using a slotted spoon and drain on paper towels to remove excess oil.

    Serve hot with your favorite chutney or dipping sauce, like mint chutney or tamarind chutney.

Tips:

  • For extra crispy samosas, double fry them. Fry them once at a lower temperature (around 160°C/320°F) until slightly golden,

    then fry them again at a higher temperature (around 180°C/350°F) for a final crisp.

  • If the dough feels too dry, add a little more water while kneading. If it feels sticky, add a little more flour.

  • You can adjust the amount of chili powder depending on your desired level of spice.

  • Feel free to experiment with different fillings! Some variations include minced meat, lentils, or a combination of vegetables.
