Chicken Dum Biryani

Description

Dum Biryani- Dum Biryani is a traditional Indian dish known for its rich flavors and aromatic spices.

It consists of fragrant basmati rice cooked with marinated chicken (such as chicken, mutton, or prawns),

along with caramelized onions, yogurt, and a blend of spices like cloves, cinnamon, cardamom, and bay leaves.

The dish is cooked in layers, with the meat and rice slow-cooked together in a sealed pot (dum) to allow the flavors to meld perfectly.

The result is a delicious and flavorful biryani that is a favorite at festive occasions and gatherings.

Ingredients:

For the Marinated Chicken:

  • 1 kg bone-in chicken pieces (thighs, drumsticks)
  • 1 medium onion, thinly sliced
  • 1 green bell pepper, chopped
  • 1 inch ginger, grated
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon garam masala powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon red chili powder (adjust to your spice preference)
  • 1/4 teaspoon coriander powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon cumin powder
  • 1 cup plain yogurt
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • Salt to taste
  • Cilantro leaves, chopped (for garnish)

For the Biryani Assembly:

  • 2 tablespoons ghee or vegetable oil
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 2 cloves
  • 2 green cardamoms
  • 1 black cardamom (optional)
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • 2 cups Basmati rice, rinsed and soaked for 30 minutes
  • 1/4 cup fried onions (optional)
  • 1/4 cup chopped cashews or almonds
  • 1/4 cup golden raisins
  • Salt to taste

Instructions:

  1. Marinate the Chicken:

    In a large bowl, combine the chicken pieces, onion, bell pepper, ginger, garlic, garam masala powder, turmeric powder,

    chili powder, coriander powder, cumin powder, yogurt, vegetable oil, and salt.

    Mix well and coat the chicken pieces thoroughly. Cover the bowl and marinate in the refrigerator for at least 2 hours,

    preferably overnight for deeper flavors.

  2. Cook the Rice:

    In a separate pot, bring a large amount of salted water to a boil.

    Add the rinsed and soaked Basmati rice and cook until it's about 80% done (slightly firm to the bite). Drain the rice and set it aside.

  3. Assemble the Biryani:

    Heat ghee or vegetable oil in a heavy-bottomed pot or Dutch oven suitable for oven use.

    Add the bay leaf, cloves, green cardamoms, black cardamom (if using), and cinnamon stick. Saute for a few seconds until fragrant.

  4. Brown the Chicken:

    Remove the marinated chicken from the yogurt mixture and add it to the hot oil in the pot.

    Sear the chicken pieces on all sides until golden brown.

  5. Add Remaining Marinade:

    Pour the remaining yogurt marinade over the browned chicken pieces in the pot.

    Bring to a simmer and cook for about 10 minutes, allowing the flavors to develop.

  6. Layer the Biryani:

    Spread half of the cooked rice over the chicken and sauce in the pot. Season with salt.

    Top with half of the fried onions (if using), cashews, and golden raisins.

  7. Repeat the Layers:

    Add the remaining rice, followed by another layer of salt, fried onions (if using), cashews, and raisins.

  8. Seal the Pot:

    Cover the pot tightly with a lid. You can also create a seal with foil placed over the lid to trap steam.

  9. Dum Cooking:

    Place the pot in a preheated oven at 200°C (400°F) for 20-25 minutes.

    This slow cooking allows the flavors and aromas to blend beautifully.

  10. Rest and Serve:

    After cooking, remove the pot from the oven and let it rest for another 10 minutes with the lid on.

    This allows the rice to absorb the remaining flavors. Gently fluff the rice with a fork before serving.

  11. Garnish and Enjoy:

    Garnish the Chicken Dum Biryani with chopped cilantro leaves and serve hot with raita (yogurt-based condiment) or a side salad.

Tips:

  • If using boneless, skinless chicken pieces, adjust the cooking time accordingly as they cook faster.

  • For a richer flavor, caramelize the onions before adding them to the pot.

  • You can substitute the vegetables in the marinade with chopped tomatoes for a tangy twist.

  • Remember, spice is subjective! Adjust the amount of chili powder to your desired level of heat.
